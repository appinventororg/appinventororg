Lesson Plan 3.2 Location-Aware Apps

Printable Version

Objectives

  • Learn about GPS and location sensing
  • Learn how to determine the current location of the device.
  • Learn about the WebViewer and how to show a map in your app.
  • Learn how to build URLs (e.g., to maps) dynamically

Outline

1. Send the students out to try an app that lists GPS coordinates.

2. Students build “Android Where’s My Car” tutorial

3. Location: Conceptualize Worksheet

4. Location: Customizations

Preparation

Location-aware apps can be tricky. You have to make sure the device’s location settings are on, and that you test the app in a place that can get a reading. Sometimes you need to restart the device to get it to start sensing location. Be sure and play around with the Android settings and sensing in your classroom setting before class.

Print out copies of the Conceptualize and Customize worksheet.

https://docs.google.com/document/d/1b67_OlKzYfoUPOhHEUw_ivOiFxBXGX3BMpD08ueZWAM/edit

1. Send the students outside to try out a location-aware app (20)

  • Have the students install the Where Am I? app, then go outside and walk around and see how the data changes. Let them know that the distance setting is an approximation of how far they are from Prof. Wolber’s office at USF.  link link
  • Alternatively, have them build it from the first sample app in the learning nugget on location: http://www.appinventor.org/location. It just displays latitude, longitude, and street address.
  • When the students get back, display the code for the app and talk about how LocationSensor and LocationSensor.LocationChanged works, and the latitude, longitude, and CurrentAddress properties. You can also introduce the distance procedure, basically just saying its a named sequence of blocks that approximates the distance between two points, that we’ll talk about procedures later, and that the formula was just grabbed from the web somewhere.

2. Students build Android Where’s My Car Tutorial (30)

The tutorial is at: http://www.appinventor.org/AndroidCar2

They’ll get experience with the LocationSensor component and another look at persistent data.

3. Location: Conceptual Worksheet(30)

Hand out the document below and ask the students to explore the questions. Depending on the age/sophistication of your students, you may want to spend more or less time on discussing what GPS is, playing with Google Maps, etc. In general it is really fun to explore this and it adds some interesting non-code technology work.

The last couple of questions are about coding including LocationSensor and building URLs. A key skill to learn is how to build a URL out of static and dynamic parts, e.g., using join to combine the first part of a Maps URL (http://maps.google.com?q=) with the current latitude and longitude (and putting the comma in-between).

After a few minutes, lead a full-class discussion of the questions.
