Animation

Animation is an optical illusion. The flipbook below is displaying a series of pages quickly so that, to the naked eye, the images are moving.


When we drive, we think of speed in terms of miles per hour (MPH). In animation and film, frames per second (FPS) is used instead, where a "frame" is each page in the flipbook.

Notice that the ball is slow and lags. This is because the Clock.Timer event triggers every 1000 milliseconds by default. You can change the Clock.TimerInterval to make an animation faster or slower-- this is how you control the frames per second.

For instance, if we changed the TimerInterval in the Clock’s properties to 10 ms instead of 1000 ms, we'd get a faster ball movement:


Now the ball moves smoothly across the screen because it is moving 1 pixel every 10 ms.


What is the frames per second (FPS) of the animation if the Clock.TimerInterval is set to 10 ms.

The Clock.Timer event will trigger every 10 ms. 10 ms is 1/100 of a second, so this means the Clock.Timer event will trigger 100 times a second. Thus the FPS is 100.
