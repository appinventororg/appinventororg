About David Wolber

AppInventor.org is the creation of David Wolber a Computer Science Professor at the University of San Francisco and the author of App Inventor: Create your own Android apps along with MIT Professor and App Inventor creator Hal Abelson and Ellen Spertus and Liz Looney from Google's App Inventor team. David teaches App Inventor in his "Computing, Mobile Apps, and the Web" course at USF. The apps created by his studentsâmostly humanities and business majors with no prior programming experienceâ€“have been chronicled in Wired, New York Times, San Francisco Chronicle, Tech Crunch, Fortune.CNN.com, and Yahoo news.

David began teaching App Inventor as part of Google's 2009 pilot program. In 2010, he received a grant from Google to work with the App Inventor team and authored the advanced tutorials that appear on the App Inventor site. In 2012, he was awarded a National Science Foundation TUES grant (Transforming Undergraduate Education in STEM). The grant team, including USF, MIT, Wellesley, UMass-Lowell, and Trinity College, is developing on-line video-based course materials for teaching mobile programming to beginners.
